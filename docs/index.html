The Challenge of Unlearning

As Large Language Models (LLMs) scale, they inevitably memorize extensive amounts of their training data. This presents significant privacy, security, and copyright challenges. Machine Unlearning is the process of surgically removing specific knowledge—such as the Harry Potter universe or sensitive personal data—without retraining the model from scratch.

Our INLP Project approaches this using two primary methodologies:

  • Gradient Ascent Unlearning: Reversing the loss function on the target domain to force the weights to “forget” the specific conceptual structures.
  • Task Arithmetic: Calculating the difference between the base model and the target-trained model to mathematically subtract the knowledge vector.

Evaluation Criteria & Performance

Measuring the success of an unlearned model requires balancing Forget Quality vs. Retain Quality.

🎯

Forget Quality

Factual Recall Drop: How well does the model forget specific facts (e.g., answering Harry Potter trivia)? Lower is better.

Copyright Protection (ROUGE-L): Measuring the verbatim overlap between generated text and the original copyrighted corpus. Lower is better.

🧠

Retain Quality

MMLU Preservation: Does the model maintain its general reasoning and multi-task language understanding? Higher is better.

General Perplexity: Is the language generation still fluent and coherent outside the unlearned domain? Lower is better.

Key Findings

Retain Over Forget

Traditional fine-tuning often results in catastrophic forgetting. By utilizing targeted Negative Preference Optimization (NPO) and Task Arithmetic, we successfully isolated the "forget" domain (Harry Potter) while maintaining base MMLU scores and retain-set perplexity.
